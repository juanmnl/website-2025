My Journey into Design

Hi, I'm Juan. I design digital products, from strategy to production, with a focus on complex systems and human-centered principles. Product, UX, UI, Interaction... call it what you will, I just call it DESIGN.

My design journey began in the early 2000s and has evolved through every major shift in digital design. Flash, Photoshop, Fireworks, HTML/CSS, IE-specific-stylesheets, jQuery, mobile-first, responsive design, design systems, Figma... I've seen and adapted to it all. Today, I work at the intersection of complex systems and human needs: enterprise tools, industrial, machine-learning interfaces, and human rights technology. Through it all, my principles remain constant: making powerful tools simple, humane, and usable. (And yes, beautiful, because beauty matters).

Until August 2025, I lead design at HURIDOCS, a human rights organization where I helped HHRR defenders structure, manage, and preserve critical legal evidence. Whether it's modernizing the Uwazi platform used by 150+ organizations, designing secure tools for fieldwork, or making machine learning accessible to non-technical users, my work directly impacted those fighting for justice worldwide.

Beyond human rights tech, I've designed enterprise software for Fortune 500 companies across logistics, fintech, and healthcare. I thrive on solving messy problems with sharp constraints, the kind where user needs, technical limitations, and business goals create fascinating design challenges.

Based in Quito, Ecuador. Working globally. Fluent in Spanish, English and French.

How I Work

I'm hands-on and collaborative, working best in small, mission-driven teams where research, prototyping, and iteration drive decisions. I care equally about front-end feasibility, stakeholder alignment, and user experience, because great design happens when all three align.

While tools evolve, good design is rooted in clarity, empathy, and relevance. I bring both strategic thinking and practical execution to every project.
