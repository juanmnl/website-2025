Project 1 Title: Solving [Specific Problem]

This project focused on improving the user onboarding experience for a B2B SaaS platform, aiming to increase activation rates by 20%.

Role

Lead UX Designer

Team

Product Manager, 2 Engineers, 1 Marketing Specialist

Timeline

6 Weeks

Tools

Figma, Miro

The Challenge

Users were dropping off significantly during the initial setup of the platform. Qualitative research revealed confusion around key features and a lack of clear guidance.

Initial user flow showing high friction points.

My Process & Research

I initiated this project by conducting user interviews with 10 new sign-ups to understand their mental models and pain points. Concurrently, I analyzed onboarding analytics to pinpoint specific drop-off stages.

  • User Interviews: Uncovered expectations vs. reality.
  • Competitor Analysis: Studied best-in-class onboarding patterns.
  • Journey Mapping: Visualized the current frustrating user path.
  • Persona Development: Identified core user types and their needs.

Key insights from user interviews and persona development.

Ideation & Design

Based on research, I led brainstorming sessions to generate solutions. We focused on simplified steps, clearer language, and contextual help.

Key design decisions included:

  • Introducing a multi-step progress indicator.
  • Breaking down complex forms into smaller chunks.
  • Implementing tooltips and short tutorial videos.

Early wireframes exploring new flow variations.

High-fidelity designs showcasing the simplified onboarding experience.

Testing & Iteration

We conducted unmoderated usability tests with 15 participants using a clickable Figma prototype. Key findings led to iterative improvements, such as refining microcopy and adjusting the placement of critical information.

Clickable prototype used for usability testing.

The Solution & Impact

The new onboarding flow significantly improved user activation. We observed a 25% increase in successful account setups within the first month post-launch, exceeding our initial goal. Qualitative feedback indicated users found the process much more intuitive and less intimidating.

The final redesigned onboarding flow.

Learnings & Next Steps

This project reinforced the importance of continuous user feedback. We learned that even small changes in microcopy can have a significant impact on user understanding. Future iterations would involve A/B testing different call-to-action button styles.
